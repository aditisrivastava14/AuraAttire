Lehenga

A royal and festive attire symbolizing elegance and celebration.

What is a Lehenga?

A lehenga is a traditional Indian outfit consisting of three main pieces: a long skirt (lehenga), a fitted blouse (choli), and a dupatta. It is widely worn during weddings, festivals, and special occasions, known for its rich fabrics and intricate designs.

History

The lehenga has its roots in the Mughal era, where it was worn by royal women. Over time, it evolved into a popular outfit across India, especially in northern regions like Rajasthan and Gujarat. It represents luxury, royalty, and cultural heritage.

How it is Worn

The lehenga skirt is worn around the waist, paired with a choli (blouse) on the upper body. A dupatta is draped over the shoulder or head, adding elegance to the outfit. The style can vary based on occasion and personal preference.

Regional Variations

  • Rajasthani Lehenga: Known for mirror work and vibrant colors.
  • Gujarati Lehenga: Features heavy embroidery and traditional patterns.
  • Bridal Lehenga: Richly designed with heavy fabrics and intricate detailing.
  • Modern Lehenga: Fusion styles with contemporary cuts and lighter fabrics.

Modern Relevance

Today, lehengas are a top choice for brides and festive wear. Designers experiment with new fabrics, colors, and styles, making lehengas suitable for both traditional and modern occasions.

Did You Know?

  • Lehengas can weigh several kilograms due to heavy embroidery.
  • Bridal lehengas are often custom-made and take weeks to design.
  • Lehengas are popular not only in India but also globally for weddings.
