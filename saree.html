Saree

A symbol of grace, tradition, and timeless Indian culture.

What is a Saree?

A saree is a traditional Indian garment consisting of a long piece of cloth, usually around 5 to 9 yards in length, draped elegantly around the body. It is typically worn with a blouse and a petticoat. Known for its versatility, the saree can be styled in multiple ways depending on the region and occasion.

History

The saree dates back over 5,000 years to the Indus Valley Civilization. It has evolved over time while maintaining its cultural significance. Ancient texts and sculptures depict women wearing draped garments similar to modern sarees, making it one of the oldest surviving forms of clothing in the world.

How it is Worn

The most common style of draping is the Nivi style, where the saree is wrapped around the waist, with pleats tucked in the front and the pallu draped over the shoulder. Other styles vary across regions and add uniqueness to the attire.

Regional Variations

  • Kanjeevaram Saree: Originating from Tamil Nadu, known for rich silk and temple borders.
  • Banarasi Saree: From Varanasi, famous for intricate gold and silver zari work.
  • Cotton Saree: Lightweight and comfortable, popular for daily wear.
  • Mysore Silk Saree: Known for smooth texture and vibrant colors.

Modern Relevance

Today, sarees are worn during weddings, festivals, formal events, and even in modern workplaces. Designers have introduced contemporary styles, fabrics, and draping techniques, making sarees a blend of tradition and modern fashion.

Did You Know?

  • A single saree can be draped in over 80 different styles.
  • Handwoven silk sarees can take weeks or even months to create.
  • Sarees are considered a symbol of grace and cultural pride in India.
